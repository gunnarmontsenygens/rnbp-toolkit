# Restricted N-Body Problem in Jacobi Coordinates Toolkit

## Overview

The **Restricted N-Body Problem in Jacobi Coordinates Toolkit** extends the **N-Body Problem in Jacobi Coordinates Toolkit** by introducing a **massless particle** whose motion is governed by the gravitational field generated by a system of massive bodies.

The massive bodies evolve according to the gravitational N-Body Problem, while the restricted body experiences their gravitational attraction without influencing their motion.

Using Jacobi coordinates allows the translational motion of the massive-body barycenter to be eliminated while preserving a compact and efficient formulation of the restricted equations of motion.

The toolkit was developed as part of **ASEN 6062 – Celestial Mechanics** at the University of Colorado Boulder.

---

## Features

The toolkit includes routines for

- propagating the Restricted N-Body Problem,
- constructing and manipulating Jacobi state vectors,
- transforming between barycentric and Jacobi coordinate systems,
- reconstructing barycentric trajectories,
- computing conserved quantities of the massive-body system,
- visualizing restricted trajectories in two and three dimensions,
- post-processing numerical simulations.

---

## Mathematical Background

The implementation assumes

- an arbitrary number of massive bodies,
- one massless particle,
- normalized gravitational constant

```text
G = 1
```

The massive bodies evolve according to the general N-Body Problem, while the restricted particle satisfies the corresponding restricted equations of motion.

---

## Relationship to the N-Body Toolkit

This toolkit should be viewed as an extension of the **N-Body Problem in Jacobi Coordinates Toolkit**.

The general N-Body toolkit provides the mathematical framework for

- Jacobi coordinate transformations,
- propagation of the massive-body system,
- conserved quantities,
- visualization.

The Restricted N-Body toolkit builds upon this foundation by introducing a massless body whose dynamics are computed relative to the evolving massive-body system.
